Software Engineer 1988 - 2000

Hardware Interface, Retail and Enterprise Software

The Bristol Group, Inc 1993 - 2000 The company had developed enterprise software on the Sun Microsystem platform to allow employees of large companies to send faxes using software through their server. They needed someone to develop the Windows PC client side version of the software. I used the socket interface, before the more standardized versions were developed to communicate with the server software and continuued to create a printer driver that would allow windows users to send faxes from any installed software. This software is still used in various applications in companies in the U.S. and in Europe.

Berkeley Systems 1991 - 1993 This company had developed a wildly popular screen saver for the Macintosh computer. I was brought in to work with outside Software Engeineers to prepare a Windows version of the software. I kept up communications with management and the outside engineers developing a screen saver for Windows that had all the same features and hooks as the Apple version. We cucceeded in creating and bringing to market the software, attended many conferences and sold millions.

The Pixellite Group 1990 - 1991 This company needed a Microsoft Windows Software Engineer to get their popular Print Artist Software compatible with Windows. I was the lead in porting the software from the DOS environment. The software was packaged and eventually sold to Sierra Entertainment.

Sapien Technologies, 1989 - 1990 This small company had partnered with a medical doctor to develop software to analyze movements related to golf swings and issues with knee mobility. I worked on the knee portion to translate information derived from a mechanical device into useful graphs and real time tracking of data. I worked under a lead engineer who gave me specific tasks. He eventually depended on me to manage the knee portion of the project entirely. Went on to work on other preliminary projects in the burgeoning Windows development environment for PCs.

Aeroteck Engineering 1988 - 1989 Small research hub needed a Turbo Pascal Programmer to help develop a software interface for controlling lasers in the laboratory. I worked under a lead programmer to complete specific tasks. Was highly successful and left to complete my degree at University of California, Berkeley.
